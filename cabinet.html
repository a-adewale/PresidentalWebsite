Cabinet

Federal Executive Council Directory

A structured overview of key offices and ministerial portfolios that support policy direction, coordination and implementation across the Federal Government.

Office / PortfolioSummary
President of the Federal Republic of NigeriaChair of the Federal Executive Council and head of the executive branch, responsible for national policy direction, executive leadership and coordination of government priorities.
Vice President of the Federal Republic of NigeriaSupports the President and leads assigned economic, intergovernmental and strategic responsibilities, including coordination with federal institutions and sub-national partners.
Secretary to the Government of the FederationCoordinates policy implementation, executive documentation, cabinet processes and communication across ministries, departments and agencies.
Head of Civil Service of the FederationLeads the federal civil service, administrative reforms, workforce standards and public-sector performance improvement programmes.
National Security AdviserProvides strategic advice and inter-agency coordination on national security, intelligence cooperation, emergency preparedness and safety priorities.
Chief of Staff to the PresidentSupports the President’s office through executive scheduling, advisory coordination, correspondence control and operational follow-through.
Minister of Finance and Coordinating Minister of the EconomyOversees fiscal policy, public finance management, revenue strategy, economic coordination and financial-sector reform priorities.
Minister of Budget and Economic PlanningCoordinates national planning, budget preparation, development priorities, performance tracking and medium-term economic frameworks.
Minister of DefenceSupports national defence policy, military administration and coordination of defence-sector priorities in partnership with security institutions.
Minister of WorksOversees federal road infrastructure, bridges, maintenance programmes and priority construction projects across the country.
Minister of Health and Social WelfareCoordinates public health policy, healthcare delivery improvement, disease prevention, health insurance priorities and social welfare programmes.
Minister of EducationLeads policy for basic, secondary, tertiary and technical education, including access, quality improvement and skills development.
Minister of Agriculture and Food SecuritySupports agricultural productivity, food security, rural development, value chains and farmer-focused programmes.
Minister of Communications, Innovation and Digital EconomyCoordinates digital infrastructure, innovation policy, connectivity, technology adoption and digital-skills programmes.
Minister of Justice and Attorney-General of the FederationProvides legal advisory services to government, supports justice-sector reform and represents federal legal interests.
Ministerial PortfoliosFederal ministries oversee national sectors including finance, works, health, education, defence, agriculture, justice, transport, power, housing and communications.
